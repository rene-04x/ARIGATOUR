Here are some Tourist Spots in Japan that you don't want to missed out!

Lake Kawaguchiko

It is one of the tourist spot in Japan, it's famous for its stunning views of Mount Fuji, which is one of Japan's most iconic landmarks. The lake is known for its picturesque scenery, with Mount Fuji serving as a breathtaking backdrop. Visitors can enjoy various activities such as boat rides, fishing, and hiking around the lake. It particularly shines during cherry blossom season. Head to the seaside promenade near the Kawaguchiko Music Forest (so named for its antique music-box museum) for views of Mount Fuji and cherry blossom trees—a postcard-worthy sight if we ever saw one.

Osaka Castle

It is arguably Osaka's most prominent landmark and belies bloody power struggles leading up to the 1603 foundation of the Edo era. While its history dates back to 1583, the landmark main tower that is the castle's icon was only re-constructed in 1931.Osaka Castle is a great place to explore as it symbolizes Osaka's culture and history. It is charming, relaxed city best known for its food, fun and nightlife—with some history and culture peeking through. Osaka is only a short shinkansen ride from Tokyo, but has a very different personality to Japan's capital city.

Tempozan Giant Ferris Wheel

If you're looking for places to visit in Osaka, you shoukd probably go here, and enjoy some of the most spectacular views of Osaka, especially the port area and the Tempozan Harbour Village. Ranking amongst some of the largest Ferris wheels in Asia, the Tempozan Giant Ferris Wheel stands tall at a staggering 112 metres, with a diameter of 100 meters. Tempozan Giant Ferris Wheel and its surrounding area offer not only beautiful views of Osaka Bay but also a variety of attractions, shopping, and dining options. It's a particularly popular spot for families and couples, and a must-visit when in Osaka.

One Piece Mugiwara Store

If you love One Piece, you have to check out the One Piece Mugiwara Store. The store has multiple locations but a new one just opened up in Shibuya, right near the Shibuya crossing! The store is full of all the One Piece merchandise you could dream of as well as life-size figures of your favourite characters for you to take pictures with! The lasting popularity of "One Piece," the unique goods, the engaging experiences, and the sense of community it offers fans all contributed to the Mugiwara Store's transformation into an important tourist destination.

Kyoto

One of the few cities in the country to be spared the devastation of WWII - attracts more than 10 million visitors annually. Most of them are here to explore Kyoto's fine old streets and architecture, much of it unchanged since the Imperial family took up residence here more than 1,000 years ago. Kyoto is home to beautiful natural scenery interwoven with historical buildings and traditional architecture. At the same time, Kyoto is a city of learning and creation, with a high concentration of cutting-edge universities, research institutions, and companies.

Japan's Okinawa archipelago

consists of more than 150 islands that speckle the area between Taiwan and Japan's mainland. This tropical environment is completely unique to other areas of Japan, home to beautiful beaches and swaying palm trees. The main island is also called Okinawa, and is home to several museums, as well as the Churaumi Aquarium. As part of the Ocean Expo Park, the aquarium offers visitors access to additional attractions, making it a comprehensive destination.

Kenrokuen Garden

It is the most beautiful garden in Kanazawa,Japan.The grounds used to be a part of Kanazawa Castle and were opened to the public in the 19th century. A Japanese Garden is not just about looking pretty. They bring out the true beauty of nature and are designed in such a manner as to make the observer look around and notice the details. A design concept utilized by the Japanese is incorporating the extended views into their design.

island of Tsunoshima

It is connected to the mainland by the massive 1,780-meters-long Tsunoshima Bridge.Though the island is small at just four square kilometers, the turquoise waters and white beaches here more than make up for its size. Since its opening in 2000, Tsunoshima Ohashi has gained popularity as a location often used in television dramas, commercials, and is well known as one of the most visited bridges in Japan. The Tsunoshima Lighthouse on the other side of the bridge has become a popular tourist destination as well.

Shinobi-zato

It is NARUTO x BORUTO collaboration theme park.located in a huge park called Nijigen-no-mori, which translates to '2-D forest' in Japanese. Nijigen-no-mori is in Awajishima, an island near Kobe. So if you are planning a trip to the Osaka area, this place might be one of the destinations! One of the highlights of Shinobi-zato is definitely Hokage Rock standing in the middle of the park. As a huge fan of Naruto, this just simply got me! It stands in front of the maze, which is one of the missions I will be talking about in a little bit. Naruto Park in Japan is a paradise for fans of the beloved anime series. From immersive attractions to themed shows, it brings the ninja world of Naruto to life in vibrant detail. With interactive experiences and memorable photo opportunities, it's a must-visit destination for fans of all ages.
